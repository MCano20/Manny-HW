Fallout

What is it?

Radioactive particles that are carried into the atmosphere
after a nuclear explosion or accident and gradually fall
back as dust or in precipitation.

Two types of radiation

Thermal Radiation

A single nuclear explosion can generate an intense pulse
of thermal radiation that can start fires and burn skin
over large areas. In some cases, the fires ignited by the
explosion can lead into a firestorm, preventing the escape
of survivors. Though difficult to predict accurately, it is
expected that thermal effects from a nuclear explosion
would be the cause of significant casualties.

Inital Radiation

Nuclear detonations release large amounts of neutron and
gamma radiation. Relative to other effects, initial radiation
is an important cause of casualties only for low-yield
explosions (less than 10 kilotons).

Aftermath

When a nuclear detonation occurs close to the ground
surface, soil mixes with the highly radioactive fission
products from the weapon. The debris is carried by
the wind and falls back to Earth over a period of minutes
to hours.
